A Quick Stop at Peppa Pig Theme Park

Home | Trip Reports | Rankings | Photos | Other Links

I spent a few minutes at this brand new children's park before heading over to Legoland for the rest of the day.


Overview

My visit to this small, cute, kiddie park lasted about 40 minutes, from 9:45AM-10:25AM. I rode Daddy Pig's Roller Coaster twice, the walked around for a bit and rode a couple other rides. This park is really neat for young children, and I'm glad that it's only steps away from Legoland. I felt a little out of place as a childless adult, but enjoyed my visit. Attraction-wise, Daddy Pig is the star.


Coaster

I don't have much to say about Daddy Pig's Roller Coaster. It's charming and decently-themed but completely forceless. I appreciate that each cycle is two laps long. There was no line on the mornning of my visit. Kid riders seemed to have a blast!


Other Rides

I rode Peppa Pig's Balloon Race and Grampy Rabbit's Dinosaur Adventure.


Food & Merchandise

I didn't purchase food or merch at this park, but they have dining locations and a gift shop.


Cost

I bought a Legoland and Peppa Pig combo ticket from CityPass for $103 total. Buying directly from Legoland's website would've cost $21 more, so I opted for the third-party option and had no issues. Check out the cost breakdown of my entire trip here.
